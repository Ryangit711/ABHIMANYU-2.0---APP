## 3. HIRING PROCESS REVEAL — Brex

Brex is a high-expectation, fast-moving fintech (~1,100 employees). Their experienced hire process for BizOps/Senior Manager roles:

**Stage 1 — Application Screen (1-3 days)**
- Recruiter reviews → technical phone screen if you pass
- ATS: Greenhouse. Clean DOCX, standard format, 2-4% keyword density
- Resume parsed by GH parser — standard rules apply

**Stage 2 — Recruiter Screen (30 min)**
- Why Brex? Why BizOps? What's your technical depth?
- Be ready to discuss specific systems you've built
- Focus: ownership mentality, cross-functional scope, AI interest

**Stage 3 — Technical Phone Screen (45-60 min)**
- This is the key gate. Expect:
  - Systems design / architecture discussion
  - Process improvement case (they give a Brex ops problem)
  - SQL/Python fluency check (can you talk tech credibly?)
  - "Tell me about a system you built from scratch"

**Stage 4 — On-site / Virtual Loop (4-5 hrs, 1 day)**
- Cross-functional: Product, Engineering, Operations, Data stakeholders
- Case study: "Design a system to handle X" or "How would you automate Y?"
- Behavioral: STAR questions anchored to ownership, scale, building from zero
- "One Brex" value = they test for low-ego collaboration

**Stage 5 — Founder Chat (optional, for senior roles)**
- Henrique or Pedro sometimes meet senior BizOps hires
- Tests: strategic thinking, founder empathy, builder mindset

**Stage 6 — Offer (1-2 weeks after loop)**
- Verbal → written → background check
- Background check: standard employment + education verification + credit check
- **⚠️ CREDIT CHECK RISK**: Fintech role touching Risk, Compliance, Payment Ops. Likely standard for a card-issuing company. Pass is typically "no major adverse events" not "excellent credit."

### Key Tips
- Lead with builder narrative: "I built X from zero" is their favorite story type
- Data-driven communication: bring metrics to every answer
- Memo culture: they value written clarity — longer thoughtful answers are better than short glib ones
- Anti-pattern: don't be a process bureaucrat. They want someone who builds, not someone who manages process

### Timeline

- Start date: negotiable (2-4 weeks after signing)

**Q1: Tell me about a system you built from scratch.**
*A:* "When I joined SkyflyMD, there was no infrastructure — 3 people, no SOPs, no tech stack. *A:* "I was an early adopter — using LLMs before they hit the mainstream in 2022-2023. I also used Python scripts to automate clinic scouting — scraping real estate and demographic data to identify acquisition targets across 10+ states. The principle: use AI to remove repetitive cognitive work so the team focuses on judgment calls."

**Q3: How would you approach transforming traditional operations into an AI-native organization?**
*A:* "Three layers. First, audit every operational workflow for automation potential — categorize by 'can be fully automated,' 'can be AI-assisted,' and 'requires human judgment.' Second, build the decision infrastructure: what rules can be encoded, what data needs to flow where, what escalation paths exist. Third, iterate with the team — the people doing the work know what's broken. My approach is to build the minimum viable automation, measure the impact, and expand from real usage data, not assumptions."

**Q4: Describe a time you managed cross-functional stakeholders with competing priorities.**
*A:* "During our $17M acquisition, I coordinated 8 concurrent workstreams across finance, legal, operations, IT, HR, compliance, and the acquiring team. Each had different timelines, different data needs, different risk tolerances. I built a central RAID log, established Monday/Wednesday/Friday sync cadences, and created a single source of truth dashboard. The key was translating between each stakeholder's language — legal needed risk documentation, finance needed projections, operations needed timeline certainty. We closed on time with zero operational disruption."

**Q5: What's a foundational question you'd want to answer in this role?**
*A:* "The one your JD already asks: 'How should operational work be redesigned in an AI-native world?' Most companies are bolt-on automation — layering AI on top of existing broken processes.
