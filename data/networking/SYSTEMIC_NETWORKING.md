# SYSTEMIC NETWORKING — The 4-Target Per Company Alchemy

> "Networking is the last piece. We've mastered everything else. This is the one thing I was shy of. Let's solve it."

## The Core Insight

**Most people network wrong.** They connect with 1 person (usually the HM or Recruiter) and ask for a job. That's loud, desperate, and easy to ignore.

**Right way:** Build a relationship web with 4 specific people at the company — each serving a different purpose, each receiving a different message, each unaware of the others. The web creates gravitational pull. By the time you speak to the HM, your name has already come up 2-3 times in different contexts.

```
                        ┌─────────────────┐
                        │   HIRING MANAGER │  ← The decider
                        │  (Tier 1 - HM)   │    Connects LAST
                        └────────┬─────────┘
                                 │ hears about you from ↑
           ┌─────────────────────┼─────────────────────┐
           │                     │                     │
           ▼                     ▼                     ▼
┌──────────────────┐  ┌──────────────────┐  ┌──────────────────┐
│  IMMEDIATE       │  │  PEER            │  │  RECRUITER       │
│  SUPERVISOR      │  │  (same level)    │  │  (HR gatekeeper) │
│  (Tier 2 - Sup)  │  │  (Tier 3 - Peer) │  │  (Tier 4 - Rec)  │
│  Connects DAY 3  │  │  Connects DAY 1  │  │  Connects DAY 0  │
└──────────────────┘  └──────────────────┘  └──────────────────┘
```

## The Order (Critical — This Is The Alchemy)

| Order | Day | Who | Why This Order | The Energy |
|:-----:|:---:|-----|----------------|------------|
| **1st** | T+0 | **PEER** | Safest connect. Same level, no power dynamic. They give you intel on the team, HM's style, real culture. By the time you talk to Supervisor, you already know the landscape. | "I see what you do — it's interesting." |
| **2nd** | T+0 | **RECRUITER** | Professional formality. "I applied, just letting you know." No ask. Keeps your file noticed. | "I'm organized. I follow process." |
| **3rd** | T+3 | **SUPERVISOR** | Now you can reference the Peer naturally. "I was speaking with [Peer] about [topic] — it made me think about [relevant experience]." Shows you integrate into teams organically. | "I do my homework. I talk to people." |
| **4th** | T+5 | **HIRING MANAGER** | By now, 3 people at the company know your name. The HM has likely heard it. Your connect message references the role, your conversations, and the value you bring — all without asking for the job. | "I'm already part of the ecosystem." |

## Why This Order Is Genius

1. **Peer first** = zero risk. If Peer ignores you, no one important saw it. If Peer engages, you now have insider intel.
2. **Recruiter parallel** = the application process is moving while you build relationships.
3. **Supervisor next** = you name-drop the Peer casually. Supervisor thinks "this person is already plugged into our team."
4. **HM last** = by the time you connect, the HM may have already heard: "Hey, there's this candidate who's been talking to [Peer] and [Supervisor] — seems sharp."

**The unspoken effect:** When HM asks the team "do we know this candidate?", the answer is "yeah, they connected with [Peer] and [Supervisor] — seems legit." That's the win.

## The 4 Messages Per Company

### MESSAGE 1 — To the PEER (T+0 — Send day of application)

**Objective:** Establish genuine peer connection. Get intel. No agenda.
**Tone:** Curious professional. Warm but not desperate.
**Length:** 200-300 chars max.

```
"Hey [Name] — your work on [specific project/skill] at [Company] caught my eye. I'm coming from a similar ops background (built 3→70, $17M exit in healthcare) and exploring [Company]. Would love to follow your journey."
```

**Why it works:** You're not asking for a job. You're saying "I see what you do — it's impressive." No pressure. Peers are the most responsive tier because they remember being job-seekers.

**If they accept + reply:** Keep it light. 1-2 exchanges. Ask ONE question about their work, their team, their favorite project. Listen more than you talk.

---

### MESSAGE 2 — To the RECRUITER (T+0 — Same day as application)

**Objective:** Mark your application as "real person, not bot." Humanize yourself.
**Tone:** Professional, organized, low-key.
**Length:** 150 chars max (recruiters scan).

```
"Hi [Name] — just applied for the [Role] role. My background scaling ops 3→70 and leading a $17M exit maps well. Happy to provide any context that helps. Thanks!"
```

**Why it works:** Most applicants ghost after clicking submit. A brief, professional heads-up marks you as organized and intentional. No ask — just information.

---

### MESSAGE 3 — To the SUPERVISOR (T+3 — 3 days after application)

**Objective:** Show you understand their team's challenges. Reference Peer connection naturally.
**Tone:** Respectful, prepared, slightly more formal.
**Length:** 250-300 chars.

```
"Hi [Name] — I've been learning about the [Department] team's work — had a great conversation with [Peer Name] about [specific topic]. My background scaling operations infrastructure (3→70 team, $17M exit) seems aligned with your team's focus on [Supervisor's initiative]. Would enjoy connecting."
```

**Why it works:** Mentioning the Peer by name shows you're already integrated. "Had a great conversation with [Peer]" signals social proof. The reference to their specific initiative shows you did your research.

---

### MESSAGE 4 — To the HIRING MANAGER (T+5 — 5 days after application)

**Objective:** Present yourself as the obvious candidate — without applying pressure.
**Tone:** Confident, strategic, slightly formal.
**Length:** 250-300 chars.

```
"Hi [Name] — I applied for the [Role] role and have enjoyed connecting with [Peer Name] and [Supervisor Name] about the team's work. My experience leading operations at a scaling healthcare business — from 3 to 70 people, through a $17M exit — aligns with what you're building. Would welcome the chance to chat when you have a moment."
```

**Why it works:** You name-drop 2 people (Peer + Supervisor) who the HM trusts. You show you've done the work. You present your value without asking for anything. The HM thinks: "This person is already invested in us."

## The Psychology of Not Sounding Desperate

| Do This | Not This |
|---------|----------|
| "Would love to follow your journey." | "I really need this job." |
| "My background seems aligned with..." | "I'm perfect for this role." |
| "Would welcome the chance to chat." | "Can I have an interview?" |
| Mention Peer + Supervisor casually | Come in cold with no context |
| "Happy to provide any context." | "Please consider my application." |
| Connect then engage (messages) | Connect with a job ask in the note |

**The golden rule:** Never ask for a job. Never ask for an interview. Never ask "are you hiring?" Present value, build relationship, let them come to you.

  └── If HM replied → advance to interview prep.

T+21 (21 days):
  ├── Close loop on cold contacts
  └── Double down on warm ones

| Response | What It Means | Your Move |
|----------|--------------|-----------|
| **Accepts connect + engages** | 🟢 Open door | Have a conversation. Ask about their work. Share your story naturally. |

| **Replies positively** | 🟢 Warm | Keep conversation going. Ask for a 10-min call if they seem open. |
| **Replies but busy** | 🟡 Soft positive | "Totally understand. I'll follow up in a week if that's OK." |
| **Gives referral freely** | 🏆 Win | Thank them. Apply through their referral link. Update me. |
| **Says "not hiring right now"** | 🟡 Honest | "Appreciate the transparency. Would still love to connect for future opps." |
