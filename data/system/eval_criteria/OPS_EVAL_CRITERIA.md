# OPERATIONS/CONSULTING RESUME EVALUATION CRITERIA

**Source:** Assimilated from interviewstreet/hiring-agent (HackerRank) — adapted from dev-focused scoring to ops/consulting context.

---

## Core Principle

Evaluates a resume the way a hiring manager or recruiter would: **6-second scan → YES/MAYBE/NO pile**. Every score must reference specific text from the resume. No vibes. All evidence.

---

## Categories

### 1. Scaling Narrative (0-35 points)

**What this measures:** Can the candidate tell a coherent growth story? For Aman specifically: does the resume make the 3→70→$17M journey feel inevitable, not accidental?

| Score Band | Criteria |
|------------|----------|
| **30-35** | Clear growth arc across the resume. Every role shows increasing scope (team size, budget, locations, revenue). The 3→70 journey is not just stated — it's demonstrated through progressive responsibilities, metrics, and inflection points. Reader thinks "this person scaled things." |
| **20-29** | Growth is visible but could be sharper. Metrics exist but don't connect into a narrative. Some roles show scaling, others are flat. |
| **10-19** | Growth is implied but vague. Few metrics. Responsibilities listed but scope unclear. |
| **5-9** | Titles and dates present but no sense of trajectory. Same-size teams or budgets across roles. |
| **0-4** | No growth narrative detectable. Static or shrinking scope. |

**Evidence must reference:** Team sizes before/after, revenue/P&L numbers, location counts, span of control changes.

**Critical rules:**
- A resume with flat 2→3 person teams across all roles cannot score above 10, regardless of title
- If the only growth metric appears in the summary (not in actual experience bullets), cap at 15
- Personal exits/acquisitions must explicitly state the outcome ($17M, acquired by X, etc.) — "successful exit" without numbers is worth 5 points max

---

### 2. Strategic Impact (0-30 points)

**What this measures:** Evidence that the candidate operated at a strategic (not just tactical) level. P&L ownership, board presentations, M&A, transformation initiatives, cross-functional leadership.

| Score Band | Criteria |
|------------|----------|
| **25-30** | Multiple examples of strategy-level work: P&L ownership ($X revenue), board/investor presentations, M&A/deal execution (buy-side or sell-side), org-wide transformations. Strategic verbs dominate: *directed, advised, structured, transformed, led corporate initiative.* |
| **15-24** | Some strategic elements but mixed with tactical. One P&L reference or one board mention. Strategy verbs present but not dominant. |
| **8-14** | Mostly operational/tactical. "Managed day-to-day operations." Few examples of setting direction vs executing it. |
| **1-7** | Entirely tactical/execution-focused. No evidence of strategic thinking or decision-making authority. |
| **0** | No resume data in this area. |

**Evidence must reference:** Specific strategic activities with scope — "Advised CEO on $X acquisition target", "Presented quarterly results to board of 12", "Led restructuring of 32-location network."

**Critical rules:**
- "Reported to CEO" is NOT strategic impact unless it includes what was reported and what decisions resulted
- Leading a function (Operations Director) implies strategy but must be evidenced in bullets
- MBA alone does not count — the strategy must be applied, not just studied

---

### 3. Operational Depth (0-25 points)

**What this measures:** Can the candidate actually execute? Real operations experience — systems built, processes designed, technology implemented, teams managed, problems solved.

| Score Band | Criteria |
|------------|----------|
| **20-25** | Deep, hands-on operational experience. Systems designed and implemented from scratch. Technology transformations led (not just supervised). Specific platforms, tools, workflows mentioned. Team management at scale (30+). Clear "before/after" evidence of operational improvement. |
| **12-19** | Solid operations experience but less hands-on or smaller scale. Some system-building. Managed teams. Technology mentioned but not central. |
| **5-11** | Operations experience present but generic. "Managed operations" without specifics. No systems or technology depth visible. |
| **1-4** | Minimal operations experience. Mostly administrative or support functions. |
| **0** | No operational content. |

**Evidence must reference:** Specific systems built (EHR, CRM, scheduling, billing), technologies implemented, team structures designed, processes created.

**Critical rules:**
- Generic "managed operations" with no specifics = 5 points max
- Healthcare operations experience is STRONGER if it shows technology/system-building (EHR implementation, RCM set up) — weaker if purely administrative
- The tech transformation story (from nothing to full stack) is Aman's strongest card here — it must be visible

---

### 4. Domain Alignment (0-10 points)

**What this measures:** How well the resume speaks the target company's language. Keyword density from company's language registry, cultural alignment indicators, industry relevance.

| Score Band | Criteria |
|------------|----------|
| **9-10** | Resume reads like an insider. Company's exact vocabulary woven naturally. Industry-specific terms used correctly. Cultural cues match (startup velocity, consulting frameworks, corporate structure). |
| **6-8** | Good alignment. Some company keywords present. Appropriate tone for the target industry. |
| **3-5** | Generic business language. Few company-specific cues. Could apply to any industry. |
| **1-2** | Mismatched vocabulary or tone. Industry-specific terms missing or wrong. |
| **0** | Clearly not aligned — wrong keywords, wrong tone, different industry focus. |

**Evidence must reference:** Specific phrases from the Language Registry that appear in the resume, cultural signals, industry terminology.

**Critical rules:**
- Pipe positioning matters: a C-pipe resume (consulting) uses different language than S-pipe (startup). Score against the SELECTED pipe.
- Healthcare terms in a consulting resume = alignment with healthcare practice. Healthcare terms in a fintech resume = misalignment. Context-dependent.
- Archetype consistency is part of this score: does the resume maintain the chosen archetype (A/B/C) throughout?

## Bonus Points (Max 20)

| Bonus | Points | Criteria |
|-------|--------|----------|
| **Quantified Exit** | +5 | $17M exit explicitly stated with buyer name or context |
| **MBA (Applied)** | +3 | MBA present AND strategy examples demonstrate MBA-level thinking, not just the credential |
| **Cross-Industry** | +2 | Evidence of working across healthcare AND tech domains |
| **Bilingual/Global** | +2 | Demonstrated work across US/Canada border or multiple countries |
| **Board/Investor Comms** | +1 per mention (max +3) | Specific mentions of presenting to board, investors, or executive committee |
| **Founder/Builder** | +3 | Built something from nothing (company, product, department) — distinct from "managed" |
| **Technical Leadership** | +2 | Led technology transformation personally (not outsourced) |
| **Total Cap** | **20** | No exceptions |

---

## Deductions

| Deduction | Points | Criteria |
|-----------|--------|----------|
| **Immigration/PR Language** | -5 | Any mention of work permit, visa, PR, sponsorship, "need" language |
| **Vague Bullet** | -3 per bullet | "Responsible for", "Involved in", "Helped with", "Assisted" — without specifics |
| **Missing Metric** | -2 per bullet | Bullet states an achievement without any number (%/person count/revenue/time) |
| **Archetype Drift** | -3 | Mixed archetype verbs across sections (builder then strategist then operator) |
| **Weak Summary** | -3 | Summary is generic ("results-driven professional") instead of narrative |
| **Format Violation** | -2 per violation | Wrong font, wrong margins, wrong section headers per ATS spec |
| **No Quantified Exit** | -3 | $17M exit exists but not stated anywhere on the resume |
| **Skill/Tool Gap** | -1 per gap | Skill appears in "Core Competencies" but has zero demonstration in experience bullets |

---

## Score Calculation

```
Total = Scaling Narrative (35) + Strategic Impact (30) + Operational Depth (25) + Domain Alignment (10)
       + Bonus Points (max 20)
       - Deductions (unlimited floor at 0)

Maximum possible: 120 points
Pass threshold:   70 points (recruiter would put in YES pile)
Warning:         50-69 points (MAYBE pile — needs iteration)
Fail:            <50 points (NO pile — rewrite before shipping)
```
